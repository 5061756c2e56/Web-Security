# QR Codes and Security

QR codes (Quick Response codes) are two-dimensional barcodes widely used to store and transmit information. While convenient, they present significant security risks.

## Formats and Types

### Data Types

- URLs
- Text
- Coordinates (vCard)
- Wi-Fi credentials
- Cryptocurrencies (wallet addresses)

## Security Risks

### Malicious QR Codes

Attackers can create QR codes pointing to:
- Phishing sites
- Malicious applications
- Compromised Wi-Fi networks
- Unauthorized actions

### Common Attacks

- **Phishing**: QR code pointing to a fraudulent site
- **Malware**: Automatic download of malicious software
- **Social engineering**: QR codes in public places

## Best Practices

- Verify URL before opening
- Use secure QR scanners
- Don't scan suspicious QR codes
- Verify QR code source
- Use signed QR codes for critical applications

## Conclusion

QR codes are convenient but require careful use. Always verify the destination before interacting with a QR code.
